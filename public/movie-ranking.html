My top 3 Anime Movies of all-time

Spirited Away (2001) – Studio Ghibli / Hayao Miyazaki

It's a magical coming-of-age story filled with surreal characters and lush world-
building. The animation is timeless, and the themes of identity, resilience, and environmentalism hit hard
even today.

Your Name (2016) – Makoto Shinkai

A beautiful fusion of romance, time travel, and destiny. The emotional pacing, music
(RADWIMPS!), and visuals create a gut-punch of a movie. It hits especially hard if you’ve ever missed
someone you never quite got to know.

Perfect Blue (1997) – Satoshi Kon

This psychological thriller blurs the line between reality and illusion. It’s intense,
disturbing, and thought-provoking—a deep dive into identity, fame, and mental breakdown.
